Use Cases

My original use cases that I planned for in my design document were:

  • Students who are struggling with the math concepts and need a review of the days lesson can go to the website and watch a video explanation of the lesson. The hope is that review will help them to complete their homework.
  • Students who have free time in math class, another class or at home can use the resources and games to increase their math skills. These can be used as review, enrichment, or as a way to practice/learn missing concepts.
  • Parents can use the website to review math material with or without their child to better help them with the homework. Parents can also use the website to check what homework was assigned and when the next assessment is. I will include links to both Schoology (LMS) and Infinite Campus (grades)

I supported these use cases by including links to resources that match the lesson I teach each day on the homework calendar. There are tips and resources to help prepare for quizzes and tests on the review page. Additionally I included websites that offer videos and practice for the concepts we are learning this year in math class located on the resources page. For students who want extension or review during free time or at home have plenty of options to choose from on the games page. Parents are supported with helpful information and links on the home page, homework page, resources page and the review page. I was able to support all of my use cases on my website.

The main difference between my original envision for my website and what I created was that I do not incorporate goal setting and data tracking into the website for students. Right now we do that either on paper kept in individual binders or in OneNote. I also did not add links for Desmos or Geogebra, these are resources that I use in my classroom semi-regularly and post the sites on the SMARTboard when needed, so it is not necessary to add to the website. Another thing that I did not put on this website is both a video of my teaching and a video of someone else teaching the same lesson for each day. Most students do not use the videos and I decided I would rather share my YouTube chanel with students rather than have to go in and update this on a daily basis. If I were to use this website for my actual math students I would embed my Google Calendar or Schoology Calendar so it would update the information as I input it into the calendars.

The main purpose of my website stayed the same, to help students be successful in 8th grade math and to provide support so parents can help their students however they see fit. I am happy with how my website turned out and feel it would be a great resource for both students and parents!

Recommended/Planned Changes

Based on the feedback that I have collected, I propose making the following changes.

Enhancing Credibility

  • I will list my educational background on the home page
  • I will list my years of teaching experience on the home page
  • Adding these credentials to my website will let students and parents know a little more about me and increase the credibility of my website.

External Links

  • I will update my code so that external links will open in a new window. This will make it easier for my users to use my website as they will not be directed away from it and have to figure out how to get back to it.
  • In order to change my external links I need to set the target attribute to target="_blank" on all links that I want to open in a new window.
  • I will go through my website and add this target attribute and then test my website in two different web browsers to ensure it all works correctly.

Banner Image

  • I will work on my banner image to make it clearer and easier to read.
  • Having my banner be more clear and easier to read will take away a distraction on my website. I don't want students or parents to have to struggle reading my banner.

Changes I am not making and reasons why

  • Font size - only one person mentioned the font size as being too small. I do not believe my font size is too small, it is the same size as most other websites so I am not making my font size any larger.
  • Header colors - I put all colors of my website through Web Aim's color contrast checker and they all passed. So I can appreciate that one user did not prefer the color but it is readable and so I am going to leave it as it is.

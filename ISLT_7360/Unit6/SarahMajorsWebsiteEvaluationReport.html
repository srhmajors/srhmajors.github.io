Participants

HTML Developer

Elizabeth, a classmate in ISLT 7360 Introduction to Web Development, reviewed the code of my pilot website. She did not find any errors in my code.

Observed User

My observed user was Anthony, he is a parent in the school district with a middle school son and so he was a perfect user to observe for my pilot website. He has already had a student go through the middle school that I work out and he struggled knowing what was going on in her classes and how to best help her. Anthony works in the corporate world in technology and accounting, he brings new ideas to fruition or helps others in the company do the same. He used his Samsung Galazy S8 smartphone to view my pilot website in a Chrome web browser while on cellular data. I asked Anthony to find some items on my website that most parents would be interested in.

User Testers

My first user tester was Joshua, a classmate in ISLT 7360 Introduction to Web Development and a 3rd grade teacher. Being a classroom teacher I knew that he would be able to provide helpful feedback on my website, he has many interactions with parents and students on a daily basis. He used a HP ProBood Intel Core i5 7th Gen in a Chrome web browser using a cable modem 250MB/s internet connection to access my website.

My second user tester was Mariah, a classmate in ISLT 7360 Introduction to Web Development and a 7th grade math teacher. Being a middle school math teacher I knew that she could provide valuable insight into what parents and students need to be successful in class. She used a Mac in a Chrome web browser using a cable modem to access my pilot website

Credibility 5

All agreed that they liked the navigation being at the top and bottom of each page. They also agreed that all pages were clear about what page you were on because they were clearly labeled. No one had any issues opening any of the links on my pilot website. One user thought the text color with a purple background in the header was a little dull, everyone else had positive comments on the colors used on the website.

All most everyone mentioned that the banner image is too busy and hard to read. It was also mentioned by several evaluators that it would be best if any links opened in a new window.

The observed user, Anthony, had no issues viewing my website on his cell phone, however he did comment that it is not as fluid as most mobile sites. One of the user testers, Joshua, mentioned that he like my photo on the home page but that I did not list any of my credentials or years of experience in teaching.

Recommended/Planned Changes

Based on the feedback that I have collected, I propose making the following changes.

Enhancing Credibility

  • I will list my educational background on the home page
  • I will list my years of teaching experience on the home page

External Links

  • I will update my code so that external links will open in a new window. This will make it easier for my users to use my website as they will not be directed away from it and have to figure out how to get back to it.

Banner Image

  • I will work on my banner image to make it clearer and easier to read.

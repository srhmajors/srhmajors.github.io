Sarah Majors Design Document

Data Collection

I created a questionnaire on Google forms and asked middle and high school students and their parents to take it.

Questions asked to the group

Purpose

The purpose of this website will be to offer help and resources for my 8th grade math students and their parents. Many students struggle with math and their parents are unsure how to help them once they begin on their homework at home. I want to equip parents to help their children and provide students the resources they need to be successful. Students can also use the website to find links to other resources and games to help them increase their math abilities.

Use Cases

  • Students who are struggling with the math concepts and need a review of the days lesson can go to the website and watch a video explanation of the lesson. The hope is that review will help them to complete their homework.
  • Students who have free time in math class, another class or at home can use the resources and games to increase their math skills. These can be used as review, enrichment, or as a way to practice/learn missing concepts.
  • Parents can use the website to review math material with or without their child to better help them with the homework. Parents can also use the website to check what homework was assigned and when the next assessment is.

Design Concept

  1. Audience

    2. The target audience for this website will be 8th grade math students and their parents. These students and parents attend the Park Hill School District in Kansas City, MO. These students and parents all have Internet access in their homes and the students are given a Lenovo Think Pad by the district to take back and forth from school. Students in this school district have had a computer provided to them by the district since they were in the 5th grade. The are efficient users of the device.

  2. Reference Websites

    3. Below are five websites that have good and/or bad (in my opinion) features that I will consider when creating my website.

    • I like the simpleness of this layout and color scheme (although I will use my school colors). Williamette Christian School - Middle School Math
    • This is an example of what I do not want in a website. It is too busy and cluttered. I do like the top navigation though. Mrs. Crenshaw's 6th Grade Math Website
    • I like that the pictures all have the same theme on this website. I also like that it has a very clean but professional look about it. Algebraic
    • This is another website that I do not like. I do not like that the font is too small to read in the navigation bar. I do not like the color scheme and how the text has to be encapsled in white to read it. I do not like the way the photo on the home page looks stretched. I do like that there is a search bar so if you are not sure where to find something you can search for it. Mrs. Moynihan Math 8
    • This website just confirms for me that I do not like the side columns on websites. I think they look to crowded and overwhelming. I want to make a website that is straight forward and simple but professional looking. I want it to have all the information but not be all crammed on the page. I do like the color scheme chosen and the white background. The navigation bar font is too small. Mrs. Sensenbaugh's Classroom

Presentation Information

I look forward to learning with you this year as we explore the 8th grade math curriculum. You will need a spiral notebook, pencil and your laptop for every class. 8th grade math will prepare you for Algebra 1A in high school. It is a much more rigorous curriculum than 7th grade. You will have homework almost every night. We will spend the first few days getting to know each other and building a community of math learners. It is important to me to create a safe learning environment for all students as we will be making a lot of mistakes as we learn. I will reinforce the fact that we learn so much more from our mistakes than we do from our correct answers so I want everyone to feel safe to share.

"We learn wisdom from failure much more than from success. We often discover what will do, by finding out what will not do; and probably he who never made a mistake never made a discovery” ― Samuel Smiles, The Lives of George and Robert Stephenson

Sarah Majors Design Document

Data Collection

I created a questionnaire on Google forms and asked middle and high school students and their parents to take it. I had six responses, five students and one parent.

Questions asked to the group

What would you find useful on an 8th grade math classroom website?

  • A link to the learning management system (LMS). For my school, that would be Schoology.
  • Help to study for tests
  • A section to ask questions or get help from the teacher
  • Video tutorials showing the process of how to do the math concept that we are learning
  • Review for what we have already learned

What information would you like to see on an 8th grade math teachers website?

  • Tips and tricks
  • Daily homework assignments
  • Links for extra help
  • Contact information
  • A list of what you will learn this year
  • Students grades and teacher comments (This would be a link to Infinite Campus for my district).
  • Videos to help walk you through the difficult homework problems
  • Games
  • Homework calendar with dates for upcoming tests

What content on this website would you be most interested in?

  • Math videos
  • A help section
  • Weekly lesson plan
  • Just the basics
  • Math test reviews

How often would you frequent a website like this?

  • One person said multiple times a day
  • Two people said daily
  • One person said several times a week
  • Two people said maybe once a week

What do you like about other teachers websites that made you want to visit it more often?

  • It's helpful
  • It has a lot of useful videos and extra links to help me better understand the topic
  • If it's an easy and fun way to learn
  • Includes the lesson plan for the week
  • It's colorful

What do you NOT want to see on a teachers website, or what would make you NOT want to visit it?

  • The site is difficult to navigate
  • Does not have helpful or useful information
  • Random math items that we will not be learning
  • School announcements

Are there any certain colors or design elements that are important to you on a math website?

  • Make it a color that catches your eye
  • Simple but interesting
  • Easy to navigate

Are there any certain colors or design elements that you DO NOT like on a math website?

  • Nothing too dark
  • Yellow or green
  • Pictures of students
  • Too complicated or having too much information

Purpose

The purpose of this website will be to offer help and resources for my 8th grade math students and their parents. Many students struggle with math and their parents are unsure how to help them once they begin on their homework at home. I want to equip parents to help their children and provide students the resources they need to be successful. Students can also use the website to find links to other resources and games to help them increase their math abilities.

Use Cases

  • Students who are struggling with the math concepts and need a review of the days lesson can go to the website and watch a video explanation of the lesson. The hope is that review will help them to complete their homework.
  • Students who have free time in math class, another class or at home can use the resources and games to increase their math skills. These can be used as review, enrichment, or as a way to practice/learn missing concepts.
  • Parents can use the website to review math material with or without their child to better help them with the homework. Parents can also use the website to check what homework was assigned and when the next assessment is. I will include links to both Schoology (LMS)and Infinite Campus (grades)

Design Concept

  1. Audience

    2. The target audience for this website will be 8th grade math students and their parents. These students and parents attend the Park Hill School District in Kansas City, MO. These students and parents all have Internet access in their homes and the students are given a Lenovo Think Pad by the district to take back and forth from school. Students in this school district have had a computer provided to them by the district since they were in the 5th grade. The are efficient users of the device.

  2. Reference Websites

    3. Below are five websites that have good and/or bad (in my opinion) features that I will consider when creating my website.

    • I like the simpleness of this layout and color scheme (although I will use my school colors). Williamette Christian School - Middle School Math
    • This is an example of what I do not want in a website. It is too busy and cluttered. I do like the top navigation though. Mrs. Crenshaw's 6th Grade Math Website
    • I like that the pictures all have the same theme on this website. I also like that it has a very clean but professional look about it. Algebraic
    • This is another website that I do not like. I do not like that the font is too small to read in the navigation bar. I do not like the color scheme and how the text has to be encapsled in white to read it. I do not like the way the photo on the home page looks stretched. I do like that there is a search bar so if you are not sure where to find something you can search for it. Mrs. Moynihan Math 8
    • This website just confirms for me that I do not like the side columns on websites. I think they look to crowded and overwhelming. Presentation Information

  1. Content Synopsis

    2. Based off of the information received from my survey, I have decided to include the following content areas on my website.

    • A home page that gives information about the class, the curriculum, my expectations and contact information. I will also list the links for Schoology and Infinite Campus on the home page.
    • A resources page with links to other websites that students would find useful
    • A review page that explains how to study for tests with review material
    • A homework page with the daily homework and upcoming assessments
    • A games page where students can go to review material or practice basic facts

  2. Text Outline of Website Organization

    3. To provide a consistent look and feel each page of my website will include the same banner at the top of each page, a heading just below the top horizontal navigation that will provide the name of the page, the same navigation and color scheme for each page, contact information at the bottom of each page and a link back to the home page. I look forward to learning with you this year as we explore the 8th grade math curriculum. You will need a spiral notebook, pencil and your laptop for every class. 8th grade math will prepare you for Algebra 1A in high school. It is a much more rigorous curriculum than 7th grade. You will have homework almost every night. We will spend the first few days getting to know each other and building a community of math learners. It is important to me to create a safe learning environment for all students as we will be making a lot of mistakes as we learn. I will reinforce the fact that we learn so much more from our mistakes than we do from our correct answers so I want everyone to feel safe to share.

"We learn wisdom from failure much more than from success. We often discover what will do, by finding out what will not do; and probably he who never made a mistake never made a discovery” ― Samuel Smiles, The Lives of George and Robert Stephenson
